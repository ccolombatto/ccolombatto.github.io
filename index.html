Hello! I am a Postdoctoral Associate in the Department of Psychology at Yale University, where I recently completed my Ph.D. I work primarily with Molly Crockett and Brian Scholl. Before coming to Yale, I did my undergraduate work at Duke University.

My research focuses on the visual roots of social cognition. For example, I am investigating how we can our attention is influenced by the attention of other people, and how our perceptual system is well-tuned to extract others' mental states. In another line of work, I am exploring how early visual processing may influence social impressions and moral judgment.
